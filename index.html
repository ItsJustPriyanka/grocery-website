healthy mango shake to try

Ingredients: 1 ripe mango, peeled and chopped, 1 cup of low-fat yogurt or almond milk (for a dairy-free option), 1/2 cup of ice cubes, 1 tablespoon of honey or maple syrup (optional, depending on the sweetness of the mango),

Instructions: Place the chopped mango, yogurt or almond milk, ice cubes, and honey or maple syrup (if using) in a blender. Blend until smooth and creamy. If the shake is too thick, you can add a little more yogurt or almond milk to reach your desired consistency. Taste the shake and adjust sweetness if needed by adding more honey or maple syrup. If you like, you can add a pinch of ground cardamom or cinnamon for extra flavor. Pour the mango shake into glasses and serve immediately. You can garnish with a slice of mango or a sprinkle of ground cinnamon on top for presentation.

Salad with Corn, Strawberries & Avocado to try

Ingredients: 2 ears of corn, husked, 1 cup of strawberries, sliced, 1 ripe avocado, diced, 4 cups of mixed salad greens (such as spinach, arugula, or lettuce), 1/4 cup of crumbled cheese, 1/4 cup of chopped fresh basil or mint leaves, 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil, 1 tablespoon of balsamic vinegar, Salt and pepper to taste.

Instructions: Cook the corn: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the corn and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until tender. Remove the corn from the water and let it cool slightly. Once cooled, use a knife to carefully cut the kernels off the cob. In a salad bowl, combine the cooked corn kernels, sliced strawberries, diced avocado, mixed salad greens, crumbled cheese, and chopped basil or mint leaves. Whisk together the extra virgin olive oil and balsamic vinegar to make the dressing. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the salad immediately as light lunch.

cucumber limeade chia seed drink to try

Ingredients: 1 medium cucumber, peeled and diced, Juice of 1-2 limes, 2 cups of water, 1 1/2 tablespoons of chia seeds, 2-3 tablespoons of honey or maple syrup (adjust to taste) Ice cubes, Fresh mint leaves for garnishing.

Instructions: In a blender, combine the diced cucumber, lime juice, and water. Blend until smooth. Pour the cucumber-lime mixture through a fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a large pitcher to strain out the pulp. You can press down on the pulp with a spoon to extract more liquid. Stir in the chia seeds and honey or maple syrup until well combined. Cover the pitcher and refrigerate the drink for at least 1 hour, or until the chia seeds have expanded and the drink is chilled. Before serving, stir the drink well to distribute the chia seeds evenly. Serve the cucumber limeade chia seed drink over ice cubes if desired, and garnish with fresh mint leaves for extra freshness and flavor.

6 healthy eating tips to fuel your day

1. Start your day with breakfast: regular breakfast consumption is associated with stronger cognitive performance and higher quality of life. Avoid processed breakfast cereals with added sugars.

2. Choose your cooking materials wisely: Cooking with vegetable oils and margarine can load your foods with unhealthy trans fats and saturated fats, raising cholesterol, so use them in moderation.

3. Read the label: Reading the labels on packaged foods is a step toward conscious consumption and aligning your habits with your dietary objectives. Pay attention to the nutritional value of all packaged foods, especially those advertised as healthy alternatives.

4. Change your snacking: Try to fill your pantry and fridge with nutrient-dense, healthy options like nuts, plain yogurt, and fresh fruits and vegetables to curb midday hunger.

5. Practice mindful eating: When you eat, focus on the sensory experience, observe how food makes you feel, and practice self-compassion. It can help you develop a better relationship with food and reduce stress.

6. Eat when you’re hungry: Intuitive eating is a way of looking at food that embraces eating when your internal cues prompt you to. It’s a great way to build habits that serve your specific needs.
